Perfume Picks

Features

Everything Perfume Picks does, in one place.

Your wardrobe, organized

Every bottle in one place. Track each fragrance by house, family, and status — own, tried, want, or empty. A remaining-mL meter shows how much is left in each bottle as you log wears.

Record purchase price per bottle. Filter by season, family, or status. Sort by what you have worn least recently, so the back of the shelf gets its turn.

A wear log with context

Log a wear in three taps. The app captures the date, pulls local weather automatically, and asks you for occasion and how many sprays.

Backdate up to two years to record the bottles you have already loved. Log two or three fragrances on the same day to track layering experiments, then revisit the combinations that worked.

Train My Nose — a taste profile from real behavior

A swipe interface presents scents with their notes, families, and houses. Swipe right on what you love, left on what you do not. Your profile starts to take shape within a few rounds.

The profile gets sharper with every wear you log. It is built from your real collection and your real choices — not from a five-question quiz.

What should I wear today?

The morning pick draws from the bottles you already own. It considers the local weather, the occasion you are heading into, the fragrance family you have been wearing too much of, and the bottles you have not touched in weeks.

This is the part most fragrance apps miss. Discovery is fine, but the everyday question is “what do I wear right now?” — and the answer should come from your shelf, not a shopping cart.

Cost-per-wear and Perfume Wrapped

Every wear ticks the counter on the bottle’s purchase price. The expensive bottle you reach for daily quietly becomes the cheap one. The cult-favorite niche purchase you have worn three times reveals itself for what it is.

At year-end, Perfume Wrapped recaps your year — top houses, top families, total wears, layering favorites, weather correlations, and the most-loved bottle by wears per month. Built from your own data, sharable as an image card.

Scent of the Day — private by default

Your wear log stays private unless you say otherwise. When you want to share, flip the public toggle on a specific entry and it joins the SOTD feed for the people who follow you.

Reactions and follows are the only social mechanics. No likes-as-leaderboard, no influencer machinery, no algorithmic push. Just a way for collectors to see what the people they respect are wearing this week.
